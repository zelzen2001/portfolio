Hi, I’m Zander

Welcome to my portfolio. I am a designer working at the intersection of AI, sports analytics, and interactive systems. This page introduces my vision and professional identity.

Vision

In the future, I envision data-enabling instruments to play an increasingly important role in our lives. Accessing these invisible streams of data that’s all around us can further support the quantified self, which is the trend of human search for self-knowledge through data. Here, design plays a critical role in how to make data accessible and clearly interpretable.

This makes it also a useful way to use technology. Where digital technologies also rely on the amount of user interaction to support their business model, some designs overdo it and produce highly addictive platforms that negatively impact our lives. With the right focus and ethical considerations, these technologies and digital platforms can be a real asset to improve quality of life.

All these data and technological advancements should not take away the human aspects of our activities. Where people are increasingly individualized, I think supporting collaboration is a functional and supportive way to encourage these interactions. Also, the way data is made interpretable and connects to the practical context can be informed by the end-user, who often possess the answers to the design questions.

Startups are examples of high-risk, high rewards when wanting to make an impact. On the one hand, there insecurity about if it will be a success that makes impact and can support life financially. On the other hand, that also works motivating as the results are a direct impact of the choices and efforts that I am making.

Sports are an interesting context for me. With the concentration it takes to focus on your performance and progress, it provides great entertainment and distraction in a society with pressures and demands. My personal interest here also improves my motivation to invest in learning activities and efforts. Here, I also learn the right skills and attitudes to design a business and develop digital platforms.

Professional Identity

Working on projects, I continuously evaluate the planning and process to ensure it fits the project. Where I first found planning to be hard, as it does not connect to the project anymore once something goes different than planned, I do value it, so you know what you work towards but also have the attitude to adapt to a dynamic process. Here, I also found more contact with end-users and validation by tests to be important to include throughout projects, as they can easily assess assumptions, which otherwise cannot be found in research or design exploration.

In my designer skills I have the right skills to create on the systemically design, as the communicative output level. Here, I use aesthetics and visual explorations to easily communicate ideas, which play an important input in discussions when exploring the design requirements, to more high-fidelity prototypes near the end of a project. This is also valuable in my way of working in an interdisciplinary team, where I understand my role with more consideration towards the design, and this way is easy to communicate. When designing, I can follow different processes, depending on the context and demands of the project. I try to involve users throughout the process to validate how the design can play a role in the actual market, which is supported by exploring business strategies. The user's reaction is evidence and data to make design decisions and what requirements are important to realize the design and technology.

I believe that this different expertise is important to the overall design of a product, and to both works structured while leaving room for creativity and exploration really contributes to better designs.

Connecting my design input to the overall success of a product or company works really motivates me. When placed in a huge company with minor responsibilities, this can be demotivating for me. Therefore, I aim to further develop AI Athletics as a startup, which is also the graduation project I did. While both the app and the business still need development, I also like the diversity of tasks that have to be handled. In the meantime, I am planning to combine this with a teaching job. With experience as a teaching assistant and a good understanding of how interdisciplinary teams can work together and get energy from working with others.
