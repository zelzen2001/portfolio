Expertise Areas

The overall development of all expertise areas is described below. In my experience in the design projects I did, I developed more awareness of how all these areas are connected in design. Where user and society of course entail the societal impact and awareness, I value the inclusion of users in design highly in search for validation or insights. With creativity being a core competence for exploration, the aesthetics help to communicate concepts, make attractive designs, and show how components belong to the same business through cohesion. Business and Entrepreneurship is thus formed by aesthetics and users but also rely on the realization of products and data to inform design decisions. MD&C can here thus play a role and be collected from users but is also an interesting input for visual design.

Technology & Realization

Developing my expertise in Technology & Realization has been a key focus of my master’s trajectory, complementing my design skills and broadening my approach to problem-solving. I aim to not only envision concepts but also bring them to life through concrete, functional solutions. I have learned to build and implement full applications, combining both backend and frontend development. Using languages such as Python, JavaScript, and TypeScript, I am able to create functional components myself while also collaborating effectively with developers in interdisciplinary teams. This collaboration includes discussing technical possibilities, exploring trade-offs, and integrating design intent with engineering constraints. In my designs, I strive to integrate emerging technologies that expand the interaction and evaluation potential of my products. For example, I developed a mobile app applying motion capture to analyze sprint form in athletes. This project combined object tracking for cone calibration, kinematic analysis, and visual feedback, demonstrating how technology can provide actionable insights in sports performance. A prior project applied similar tracking technology in an exergame, using wearable sensors to control a Flappy Bird–style game, highlighting how motion data can inform playful and engaging experiences. Beyond applications, I also engage directly in training AI models. One example involved drumsticks that identify sounds, where I labeled data, trained the model, and integrated it into a functional system. This experience deepened my understanding of machine learning pipelines and reinforced how hands-on experimentation informs design decisions. Through these projects, I have learned to navigate the intersection of design, technology, and user experience. I aim to combine creativity with technical realization, ensuring that my designs are not only conceptually strong but also practically implementable and meaningful for end-users. Working with both cutting-edge technologies and interdisciplinary collaborators has strengthened my ability to translate innovative ideas into tangible, functional products.

Business & Entrepreneurship

Developing my expertise in Business & Entrepreneurship has been a central part of my growth, particularly through working on my own startup, AI Athletics, which also served as my graduation project. In this context, I explored not only the functional and design aspects of the product but also its potential in the market and how it could fit into users’ routines. Engaging potential customers directly in the development process allowed me to gather real-world insights and validate assumptions continuously. To guide strategic decisions, I collaborated with business experts who helped me understand market positioning, prioritize next steps, and identify opportunities for growth. I place strong emphasis on quick validation cycles—rapid prototyping, testing, and iterating—to learn which features, approaches, and offerings are truly valued by users. On the business side, I developed skills in creating business models, conducting competitor analyses, and estimating market potential. I also gained practical experience in early-stage funding, including applying for grants and other resources essential to getting a startup off the ground. To test real willingness to pay, I implemented the concierge method, offering services manually to assess user interest and refine the value proposition before scaling. These explorative validation cycles extend beyond product features. I apply the same approach to marketing materials, experimenting with different platforms, formats, and messaging strategies to determine which methods effectively reach and engage target audiences. Through these experiences, I have learned to balance product design, user needs, and business viability. I approach entrepreneurship not as a separate discipline but as an integrated part of the design process, ensuring that solutions are both meaningful for users and sustainable in the market. This expertise area has strengthened my ability to turn innovative concepts into practical, scalable, and impactful ventures while remaining closely connected to the real-world context of end-users.

User & Society

I place great value on the role of users in design, as they possess the practical experience and knowledge that often provide answers to key design decisions. To leverage this, I have learned to plan, conduct, and analyze user tests in different contexts, gathering insights that directly inform design choices. Alongside end-users, I highly value the input of industry experts, whose experience often goes beyond what research can reveal. Their perspectives not only guide functional decisions but also shape the overall user experience. To better understand interaction patterns, I use tools such as user journey maps to compare assumptions with actual behavior, aiming to design solutions that seamlessly integrate into existing routines. In digital products, I apply principles like “overview first, zoom and filter, details on demand” to maintain clarity and structure, ensuring designs remain intuitive and manageable. I also consider the broader societal context in which designs are situated. Through projects addressing topics such as partydrug consumption, the quantified self, and the transition of students to a new city, I explore ethical considerations, societal trends, and opportunities for meaningful impact. Understanding these contexts allows me to create designs that are not only useful and usable but also socially responsible and relevant. By combining user insights, expert knowledge, and societal awareness, I aim to create solutions that are both practical and impactful, aligning design decisions with real-world needs while fostering positive experiences and outcomes for the people who interact with them.

Creativity & Aesthetics

Exploration and creativity are at the core of my design practice. I often begin with sketches and visualizations, leveraging my skills to generate multiple concepts without constraints. This exploratory phase allows me to test ideas quickly and think divergently. In the next stage, I translate concepts into functional prototypes, where creativity remains essential—not to overinvest in fully finished products, but to communicate the design intent effectively. Aesthetics are a key consideration in the final design, influencing how colors, shapes, and forms create an identity and appeal. I tend toward smooth, futuristic designs, aiming for a visual language that communicates intention while remaining approachable. Interaction design is equally important; how a product behaves and responds to users helps convey purpose and functionality. Consistency across components, including interfaces, branding elements, and subtle details, creates cohesiveness and reinforces recognition of a product or brand. The dynamic aspects of a design, movements, changes, and surprises, also contribute to user engagement and emotional response. I often link visual output to user data, creating responsive or personalized experiences that are both informative and engaging. To realize these visual ideas, I use Adobe Creative Cloud tools extensively. Illustrator and Photoshop are my main tools for static visuals, while InDesign supports reports and structured documents. For motion and interactive visuals, I use Premiere Pro for video production and After Effects for adding detail, animation, and visual storytelling elements. This skill set enables me to translate creative concepts into polished, functional, and expressive outputs that communicate both form and function effectively.

Math, Data & Computing

I see data as a fundamental component in design, acting as both a qualitative and quantitative source of insight for decision-making. Within my work on AI Athletics, I collect and process movement data using pose estimation techniques to analyze sprint performance. While this data initially consists of 2D pixel coordinates, it is calibrated and transformed into real-world measurements, enabling meaningful interpretation in a sports context. To derive actionable output for athletes and coaches, I compute biomechanical features such as joint angles, limb trajectories, and temporal movement patterns. These metrics translate raw positional data into performance indicators that are relevant to the end-user and their training goals. Given the limitations of hardware and vision-based systems, I critically evaluate data quality and apply filtering and fine-tuning methods to improve reliability. This includes removing outliers, correcting implausible values, and validating consistency across frames. I also make deliberate decisions about data storage, defining what information is relevant to retain in databases versus what can be discarded to optimize performance and efficiency. Equally important is how data is communicated. I focus on designing clear and intuitive visualizations that avoid cognitive overload. This involves selecting appropriate chart types, maintaining clear labeling, and using structured color coding to support interpretation. In addition, I often integrate data directly into generative or interactive visual outputs, where metrics drive visual behavior and enhance understanding through experience. Through this combination of data processing, computational reasoning, and visualization design, I aim to bridge raw technical data with meaningful, user-centered insights.
